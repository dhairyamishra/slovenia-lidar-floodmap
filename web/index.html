How to read the map

Each switch adds a different kind of evidence. They are screens for exploration, not a flood forecast or a single combined score.

  1. 1

    Start with the official reference. Choose Q100 for a familiar planning comparison.

  2. 2

    Add terrain susceptibility. Look for agreement or disagreement, then inspect the local setting.

  3. 3

    Add the context layer for the question. Land cover for local setting, coastal exposure for Koper, or hydroclimate for the Savinja demonstration.

Opacity tip: 30–50% is best for comparing an overlay with roads and terrain beneath it.

About

LiDAR-derived riverine terrain screening across Ljubljana, Savinja and Koper, with a Koper connected-low-land screen and a synthetic hydroclimate fixture. The riverine model combines HAND, TWI, elevation, slope, curvature, canopy interception and NDVI. The hydroclimate layer indicates temporal priming, not flood probability.

Screening only: not a forecast, evacuation map, insurance map, official hazard map, or hydraulic depth estimate.
