How to read the map

Each switch adds a different kind of evidence. They are screens for exploration, not a flood forecast or a single combined score.

  1. 1

    Start with the official reference. Choose Q100 for a familiar planning comparison.

  2. 2

    Add the experimental terrain baseline. Use its sparse review mask to inspect agreement or disagreement.

  3. 3

    Add the context layer for the question. Use land cover for local setting or coastal exposure for Koper.

Opacity tip: 30–50% is best for comparing an overlay with roads and terrain beneath it.

About

LiDAR-derived riverine terrain screening across Ljubljana, Savinja and Koper, with a separate Koper connected-low-land screen. The riverine model combines HAND, TWI, elevation, slope, curvature, canopy interception and NDVI.

Screening only: not a forecast, evacuation map, insurance map, official hazard map, or hydraulic depth estimate.
